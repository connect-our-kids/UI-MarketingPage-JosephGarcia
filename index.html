Our Story

Growing up, Jennifer Jacobs rode the school bus with neighbors who were foster children. She often wondered why their story was so dramatically different from hers. Over the years, and later as a mother, she often returned to the thought of how challenging their lives must be, moving from home to home without permanent families.

After graduating from West Point, earning a Ph.D. in nuclear engineering, and working at a nuclear weapons lab, Dr. Jacobs went on to serve as a White House Fellow on the National Security Council and later to McKinsey & Co. as a consultant to government and industry.

In 2011, Dr. Jacobs read an article in Time magazine about a successful family-finding process used in foster care. However, the labor-intensive process involved manually identifying over 150 extended family members to pinpoint a single person who was willing and able to offer a safe and supportive permanent home to a child. A team of social workers could only help a few kids a year out of the 400,000 in the system across the United States.
